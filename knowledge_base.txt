5. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
==================================================
Q1. I have leads coming from Facebook, a WordPress form and a random Google Sheet. Can you merge them into one place without duplicates?
A: Yes. I build a central n8n workflow that catches each source with a webhook or polling trigger, normalises the fields, hashes the email/phone to find duplicates, then drops the clean record into Airtable, HubSpot or Notion. Once live, you’ll never export a CSV by hand again.

Q2. My CRM is “just Google Sheets” – is that enough?
A: For up to ~50k rows it’s fine. I add data-validation, unique IDs and a simple UI with Airtable’s Interface designer or a mini Next.js portal so your team gets CRM benefits without migrating anywhere.

Q3. Can you qualify leads automatically before they book a call?
A: I add an OpenAI step that scores budget, timeline and intent from the form answers. Only hot leads create a Calendly event; lukewarm ones enter a nurture email sequence.

Q4. I record podcast audio – can it become blog posts, tweets and an email automatically?
A: Upload the mp3 -> DeepGram for transcription -> GPT-4 re-writes in three tones -> n8n routes to WordPress (blog), Buffer (tweets) and MailerLite (email). DALL·E whips up a cover image that matches the episode title.

Q5. I hate designing Instagram carousels. Any fix?
A: I vibe-code a tiny web app. You paste the text, it calls DALL·E for five square images, merges them into a PDF carousel and posts via Instagram Graph API at the hour you pick. No Canva clicks needed.

Q6. Can a WhatsApp bot pull live order status from my Shopify store?
A: I host a lightweight Express server that queries Shopify by order number, formats the reply and shoots it back through Twilio WhatsApp. Customers get instant updates 24/7.

Q7. What if customers prefer voice notes instead of text?
A: ElevenLabs converts the text answer into audio; Twilio sends it as a voice note. It feels surprisingly human.

Q8. What exactly is a RAG pipeline and do I need one?
A: RAG = your private data + vector search + AI. If staff keep asking “Where’s the SOP for refunds?” or clients want instant answers from your PDFs, RAG gives them accurate replies without you copy/pasting. I build mine with Supabase pgvector or Weaviate.

Q9. How often does the knowledge base update itself?
A: I schedule nightly syncs: new Notion pages -> auto-chunked -> embedded -> inserted into the vector table. Zero manual work.

Q10. I only have an idea – no wireframes, no brand board. Can we still start?
A: Absolutely. We jump on a 30-min call, I convert your words into a Figma wireframe with AI, then vibe-code the first version while you watch on Loom. We polish the UI as we go.

Q11. Do I own the source code?
A: 100%. Repos live in your GitHub; I transfer Vercel/Netlify projects to your account on day one.

Q12. Can you add login, payments and an admin panel to a vibe-coded site?
A: Next-Auth for social logins, Stripe/Paystack for billing, and a role-based dashboard auto-generated from your database schema.

Q13. I need a simple barcode-scanner app that uploads to my Airtable inventory. How fast?
A: React Native Expo app: camera scan -> API call -> Airtable row. TestFlight build within 48h.

Q14. Can the app work offline and sync later?
A: Yes. Local SQLite cache queues the scans; uploads automatically when back online.

Q15. Which database should I pick – Airtable, Supabase or Firebase?
Quick rule:
- Airtable = spreadsheet comfort + low-code automations.
- Supabase = full SQL, foreign keys, vector plug-in, scales forever.
- Firebase = real-time sync, great for chat or multiplayer.
I migrate between them without data loss.

Q16. Can you clean a 50k-row messy CSV before it hits production?
A: Python pipeline: dedupe, standardize phone numbers, validate emails, geocode addresses, output ready-to-insert SQL.

Q17. How do you handle APIs with strict rate limits?
A: I queue requests in Redis, drip them just under the limit, add exponential back-off and dead-letter logging so nothing drops.

Q18. I want a Slack bot that roasts the team every Friday with project stats. Can AI be funny?
A: I feed git commits, closed tickets and revenue numbers to GPT-4 with a “roast” persona. Example: “John pushed 2 commits this week – both were typo fixes. Legend.”

Q19. Can you automate birthday surprises for employees?
A: HR sheet -> daily check -> if birthday, trigger Loom video generator (AI voice greeting), post in #general and email a Starbucks e-gift card.

Q20. I hate writing meeting minutes – help?
A: Zoom cloud recording -> webhook -> DeepGram -> GPT-4 summary -> Notion page tagged with attendees and action items. Slack bot pings owners.

Q21. Do you offer support after launch?
A: Monthly “automation clinic” retainer. You dump new pains in a shared channel; I ship fixes, usually same day.

Q22. How do I get started today?
